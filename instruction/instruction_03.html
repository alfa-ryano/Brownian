Instruction for Practice 03/h1>

In this problem, the investor has a fund of [portfolio] ECU, and he/she entrusts you to manage his/her funds. You only have two types of the market, the money market and the asset market, to invest the funds. The money market gives you a constant interest rate of [interest], whereas the asset market trades assets continuously in time. The initial price in the asset market if [price] ECU per unit asset.

The investor has his/her own strategy if he/she manages his/her fund by himself/herself. He/she will allocate some of the funds in each market in the beginning and lets the portfolio grows until the maturity period. Here, the investor allocates [b_asset] percent of the funds in the asset market - that is [1 - b_asset] percent of the funds allocated in the money market. Of course, this portfolio fluctuates over an investment period and will be the benchmark by the maturity period.

Given this information, you are asked if you can manage the funds better than that of the investor's benchmark. This will determine your compensation. If you can perform better than that of the investor benchmark by the maturity period, you will earn more compensation.

Your Compensation

You will receive compensation by managing the investor's funds. There are two types of compensation, the fixed compensation and the additional compensation. The fixed compensation is [fixed_comp] and will be paid to you independently of your performance. The additional compensation is [add_comp] percent of positive profit that you have made from your final portfolio minus the benchmark portfolio. So you will earn more compensation if your final portfolio is higher than that of the final benchmark.
