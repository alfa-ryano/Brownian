General Instruction

The Experiment

This experiment is interested in your decision as a fund manager who manages the investor’s funds. You are entrusted to manage his/or her funds and then the investor will see if you can perform better than that of the investor’s benchmark. What the investor’s benchmark will be explained later. There are two types of market that you can invest, the money market and the asset market. The money market is risk-free that gives you a constant interest rate, whereas the asset market is risky that trades assets continuously in time.

You will be presented with a sequence of 10 problems, all of the same type. Each problem has its unique circumstances depending on its initial setup. In the beginning of each problem, you are endowed by an initial fund for you to manage in a certain time. You are told the initial price of an asset in the asset market and the interest rate in the money market. Given these information, you will be then asked to allocate your portfolio in each market. This is to set your allocation in the first period in a particular problem.

You will receive compensations depending on your performance with respect to the investor’s benchmark in the end of each problem. The benchmark is a constant portfolio consisting of the proportions of the funds invested in the money market and in the asset market. These proportions are determined by the investor in the beginning of the problem. There are two types of the compensation that you will receive, a fixed compensation and an additional compensation. The fixed compensation is paid to you independently of your performance. The additional compensation is paid proportionally to the profit earned by you if the final portfolio you manage is higher than that of the final benchmark portfolio.

You will be told these information in the beginning of each problem along with other information. Given this, your compensation function is α(WT – YT)+ + K

where K is the fixed compensation and α is the proportion of positive margin of your final portfolio (WT) to the final benchmark portfolio (YT) that you have managed. All the currency used in this experiment is in ECU (Experimental Currency Unit). Therefore, you can earn more if your final portfolio is higher than that of final benchmark portfolio.

Here is to give you an example of how you will be compensated in a particular problem:

Example: You are asked by the investor if you can optimise his/her funds in both money market and asset market. The initial fund that you can manage is 100 ECU. The investor has his/her benchmark to the portfolio allocation in both money market and asset market. He/she would allocate 0% of the initial fund to the money market, and 100% of the initial fund to the asset market. The final portfolio following the investor’s allocation will be the final benchmark portfolio. The money market return is 0%, whereas the asset market trades asset in continuously period.

You will receive two types of compensation as part of your effort by the end of the problem: a fixed compensation and an additional compensation. The fixed compensation for you is 10 ECU. The additional compensation is 25% if your final portfolio is higher than that of the final benchmark portfolio. Then you are asked to allocate your initial allocation given these information, and manage your portfolio for a given certain time – as an example, the total time in this problem is 5 minutes.

If, for example, your final portfolio is 190 ECU and the final benchmark portfolio is 150 ECU by the end of a problem. Your compensation is calculated as follow: 0.25(190 – 150) + 10 = 20 ECU. You receive both fixed compensation and additional compensation because you have managed to have a higher final portfolio than that of the final benchmark portfolio.

If, for example, your final portfolio is 190 ECU and the final benchmark portfolio is 200 ECU by the end of a problem. Your compensation therefore is 10 ECU. You receive only a fixed compensation because you have failed to have a higher final portfolio than that of the final benchmark portfolio.
